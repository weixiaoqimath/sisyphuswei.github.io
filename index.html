A Very Short Note on Persistent Homology

Posted on 2020-06-10 Edited on 2020-06-13

Introduction

A filtration \(X=\{X_r | r \in \mathbb R\}\) is a series of finite simplicial complex indexed over real numbers such that each \(X_a\) is a simplicial complex and \(X_a \subset X_b\) for \(a \leq b\). Let \(X_{-\infty}= \bigcap X_a\) and \(X_{\infty} = \bigcup X_a\). In this note we focus on finite filtration. A finite filtration can be seen as a series of simplex addition, so we often represented as a list and feed it into algorithm.

For each \(X_i\) one can compute its homology. The inclusion map from \(X_i\) to \(X_{i+1}\) induce homomorphism between homology groups. These homology groups and induced homomorphisms is one example of the so called persistent module.
